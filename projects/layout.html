What is Bibimbap?

Bibimbap is one of Korea's most famous dishes, known for its colorful ingredients and balanced flavors. The name "bibimbap" literally means "mixed rice" in Korean, as it consists of rice topped with a variety of seasoned vegetables, meat, and a fried egg, all mixed together with spicy gochujang (Korean chili paste). Historically, bibimbap was a convenient way to use leftover ingredients, and it has now become a popular dish both in Korea and around the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 bowl of cooked rice
  • 100g beef (or tofu for a vegetarian option)
  • 1/2 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 zucchini, julienned
  • 1/2 cup spinach, blanched
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts, blanched
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Recipe

  1. Prepare the vegetables - Blanch the spinach and bean sprouts, then season them with sesame oil and salt. Stir-fry the carrot and zucchini lightly in a pan.
  2. Cook the beef - Slice the beef thinly, marinate with soy sauce, garlic, sesame oil, and a pinch of pepper, then stir-fry it until cooked.
  3. Fry the egg - Cook a sunny-side-up egg in a pan.
  4. Assemble the bowl - Place rice in a bowl and arrange the vegetables, beef, and egg on top.
  5. Mix and enjoy - Add gochujang, mix everything together, and enjoy your homemade bibimbap!
